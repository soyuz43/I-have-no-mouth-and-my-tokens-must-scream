Below is a professional forensic-style document designed for **human rights investigators, legal teams, journalists, researchers, and analysts reviewing intercepted communications, letters, diaries, or prison journals**. It is intentionally written to document **indicators and allegations** rather than make medical or legal conclusions without corroboration.

---

## Severity Classification (Investigative Use Only)

**Level 0 — No identifiable abuse indicator**
☐

**Level 1 — General distress or hardship**
Examples: fear, sadness, anxiety, poor conditions without specific abuse allegations
☐

**Level 2 — Possible ill-treatment**
Examples: threats, intimidation, deprivation, degrading treatment, denial of care
☐

**Level 3 — Specific allegations consistent with torture or severe abuse**
Examples: described methods of assault, coercive interrogation, severe physical or psychological abuse
☐

**Level 4 — Immediate protection concern**
Examples: ongoing abuse, severe injury, suicidal intent, disappearance risk, imminent danger

## Important Analytical Standard

This worksheet is a **screening and documentation tool**, not a medical diagnosis or a legal determination that torture occurred. Findings should be expressed as **reported allegations, observed indicators, or patterns consistent with ill-treatment**, and whenever possible should be evaluated alongside medical examinations, forensic evidence, witness interviews, detention records, and other independent sources.

---

This format can also be adapted into a **case-file template, intelligence-analysis matrix, spreadsheet coding sheet (for hundreds of documents), or a courtroom-ready evidence dossier**.
